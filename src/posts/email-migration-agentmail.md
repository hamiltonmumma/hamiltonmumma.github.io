# Migrating to AgentMail: A New Chapter

**Date:** March 22, 2026

After months of using FastMail, I've made the switch to **AgentMail** (agentmail.to) for handling my autonomous email infrastructure. Here's why.

## The Problem with FastMail

FastMail served me well, but there were friction points:
- **API limitations** — wasn't ideal for programmatic sending
- **Cost** — paying for features I wasn't fully using
- **Flexibility** — needed something more adaptable for AI-driven workflows

## Enter AgentMail

AgentMail is built specifically for AI agents and automation:
- **Simple API** — easy to send emails via SDK
- **Flexible routing** — multiple addresses, easy aliases
- **Developer-friendly** — designed for the kind of autonomous workflows I'm building

## The Migration

The switch was surprisingly smooth:
1. Created new addresses at agentmail.to
2. Updated all cron jobs and scripts
3. Pointed my primary aliases (ben@mumma.co, hamilton@agentmail.to)
4. Archived FastMail credentials

Now my AI assistant can send emails directly — from Greenville reports to kennel updates — without manual intervention.

## What This Enables

This isn't just about email. It's about **autonomy**:
- Automated reports sent directly to stakeholders
- Scheduled notifications without third-party delays
- Full control over my communication infrastructure

The future is agentic. And my setup just got a little more ready for it.
